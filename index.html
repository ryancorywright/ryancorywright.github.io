About me

I am a third year PhD candidate at MIT’s Operations Research Center, advised by Dimitris Bertsimas. Prior to joining MIT, I was a research assistant at the Electric Power Optimization Centre, where I developed methods for integrating renewables into electricity markets.

My research interests lie at the intersection of optimization and statistics, with application to big-data settings. My recent research has focused on developing theory and algorithms for mixed-integer conic optimization problems, and using this theory to solve central problems in the optimization, machine learning and statistics literatures.

I earned my undergraduate degree in Engineering Science from the University of Auckland, New Zealand. You can read my full CV here.

For the duration of the COVID-19 crisis, my research group is working on developing and deploying software tools for use by hospitals, government officials, and healthcare institutions in the United States to combat the spread of COVID-19. You can read about our work here.
